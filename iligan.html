Welcome to Iligan City

Maria Cristina Falls

Known as the “Mother of Industry,” Maria Cristina Falls powers the Agus VI Hydroelectric Plant. It's one of Iligan’s most iconic natural landmarks and symbolizes the city’s connection to energy production.

Tinago Falls

A hidden paradise surrounded by lush vegetation and cliffs, Tinago Falls is known for its cool blue waters and peaceful setting—a favorite among travelers seeking calm and nature.

Iligan City Hall

Sitting atop a hill overlooking the city, Iligan City Hall is a landmark of governance and public service. Its location provides a scenic viewpoint of Iligan.

MSU-Iligan Institute of Technology

One of the country’s top universities in science and technology, MSU-IIT is a hub of research, innovation, and academic excellence that draws students from all over the Philippines.

Paseo de Santiago

Paseo de Santiago is a seaside leisure park famous for its relaxing sunset views, open-air dining, and the iconic giant “Iligan City” letters by the bay. It's one of the city’s favorite hangout spots for both locals and tourists, offering food stalls, cultural shows, and a chill sea breeze.

Anahaw Amphitheater

The Anahaw Amphitheater, located inside the Iligan City Public Plaza, is a cultural hub known for its open-air design and iconic green leaf-shaped architecture. It hosts city events, performances, and festivals — especially during Diyandi Festival. It's also a peaceful spot for relaxation with a good view of the surrounding park.

Iligan City Night Market

The Iligan Night Market comes alive with food stalls, thrift shops, local crafts, and street performances. It’s one of the city’s busiest evening hotspots, offering affordable eats, unique finds, and a lively social atmosphere. A perfect stop for students, tourists, and locals looking for late-night food and fun.
